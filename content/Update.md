### My situation so far

Not at all a big fan of the popular web stacks I encountered (React and Node.js 🤢). So, naturally, for the longest time I thought I should stay as far as possible from the webdev world as I could. And so I did :D

### Contact with Elixir

I was one of the lucky ones at my Faculty to be accepted for a 4 day elixir summer school at the beginning of the holiday. What made me sign up when I saw the announcement was the combination of 'functional programming' and 'parallel programming', which are both 2 things I liked a lot. So I gave it a try!

The selection was made based on our github profiles (already not that good of a start). However, I had some huge blind luck here: one of the HRs responsible for selection was a friend of mine, who had recognised my name and accepted me with a more **laid back** check (he didnt **DIRECTLY** accept me, he still added me to a secondary list, since the 3rd year students had priority). When I was fully accepted after a few weeks he simply DMed me the news, saying that '*if anyone asks, you received an official email*' 🤔

### The actual experience

The theme was to create a simplified web version of **Papers Please** using **Elixir Phoenix**, with most of the UI components implemented (thank god). We started the first day with simple elixir syntax and workflow basics, which really sticked to me (Pattern matching everywhere <3). When we got to the more complex stuff, however, we actually started modifying and looking thoroughly through the entire code base, taking it slow. GenServer seemed really nice as a way to save state in a ~~purely~~ functional programming language, and the whole Supervision part felt ingenious.

By the last day I could comfidently say that I actually understood most of what was going on inside that code base, which felt amazing and gave me a huge confidenge boost. And since the last day was a short **hackathon**, i was luck to be paired with someone I had known beforehand and we were able to get 3rd place 🙏🏻 (and a mouse for me).

### Verdict

Seeing an actually cool and easy to follow web framework felt eyeopening, and I already have a project in mind to keep this elixir momentum going! 🐦‍🔥
